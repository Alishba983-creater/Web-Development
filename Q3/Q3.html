HISTORY

Abbottabad is a picturesque city located in the Hazara region of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Pakistan. It was founded in 1853 by Major James Abbott, a British army officer, after whom the city is named. Originally established as a military cantonment, Abbottabad became an important administrative and educational center during the British colonial era. Its cool climate, scenic beauty, and clean environment made it a popular hill station among the British. Over the years, Abbottabad has grown into a bustling city while maintaining its peaceful charm. It is home to several prestigious institutions, including the Pakistan Military Academy (PMA) at Kakul, which trains future officers of the Pakistan Army. Today, Abbottabad is known for its blend of colonial heritage, natural beauty, and educational excellence. It serves as a gateway to the northern areas of Pakistan and remains a favorite stop for tourists heading to Naran, Kaghan, and Hunza. More
